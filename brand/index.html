Brand Voice

Voice, Tone & Copy

Mission

Accelerating the next generation of medicine.

One-line value proposition

The fastest, most cost-effective path from therapy idea to first-in-human study.

Positioning statement

Open NeuroTech LLC is scaling the open-source COSMIIC platform to meet the demand of researchers and early-stage startups who need implantable technology.

Brand Personality

Authoritative but accessible

30+ years of credible CWRU/MetroHealth lineage and growing community, written so an early-stage founder feels invited in, not gatekept.

Concrete over abstract

Lead with proof points: 10 humans implanted, 3 FDA IDEs, 16 active pilots, $14.7M NIH program.

Open and collaborative

The word "open" is operative. The brand actively rejects IP-locked, opaque, vendor-dependent norms in implantable medical devices.

Quietly urgent

"This is where most promising therapies die" lands harder than hype because the rest of the copy is calm and specific.

Voice Principles

Open NeuroTech's voice is a senior engineer explaining a hard problem to a smart peer — direct, technically literate, comfortable with specifics, allergic to hype. The reader should feel respected, not sold to.

1
Lead with the burden, then the solution.
2
Quantify everything quantifiable. "3–7+ years and $10M+ saved" is the entire brand promise in one phrase.
3
Use plain English for the science. "Replace drugs with targeted electrical stimulation." Not "leverage bioelectronic modulation paradigms."

Vocabulary

Use regularly
open-source platform implantable modular neuromodulation large animal / preclinical first-in-human translational Investigational Device Exemption (IDE) research use only
Avoid
FDA approved FDA cleared medical device

Approved Key Phrases

Use these verbatim when possible — they form the narrative spine of the site and deck.

Mission & tagline
  • Accelerating therapies that restore function and independence.
  • Accelerating the next generation of medicine.
Value propositions
  • The fastest, most cost-effective path from therapy idea to first-in-human study.
  • The world's first open-source, fully implantable neuromodulation platform.
  • One platform across stages of development.
  • Don't recreate the wheel. Use the COSMIIC System.
Proof points — use as written
  • 10 humans implanted · 3 FDA IDEs · 16 active pilots
  • Built on 30+ years of implantable device development at CWRU and MetroHealth.
  • Led the world's first open-source implantable device through a $14.7M NIH program.
  • Saves 3–7+ years and up to $10M compared to building in-house or hiring a contract device manufacturer.
Problem framing
  • Every neuromodulation idea needs hardware to prove it.
  • This is where most promising therapies die. Not from failed science, but from the cost and complexity of getting implantable hardware.
  • Researchers need human-grade implantable tech to conduct first-in-human studies, but have minimal access and developing their own is resource-intensive and complex.
